523

29o Ja: 1629o./ [CENTRE HEADING]

Ex offo./

Robert Johnson of Saint Katherines Sailor late one of
the Company of the shippe the Unicorne of London
sworne and examined saieth as followeth,/

To the interrogatoryes hee saieth That in ffebruary
laste paste the Unicorne aforesaid ws at Gio
in the Arches pf Pelagos and there did then ride a
Neapolitan shippe of 14 peeces of ordnannse
and Mathew Wood the Captaine of the Unicorne
tooke some of his menn aboard that shippe who
tooke possession of her and kepte aboard her about
fower houres, and the Master and Company of that
Neapolitan shipp did acknowledge and confesse in
this examinats heareinge that she ws a shippe
of Naples and was good prize to Wood and his
Companye, and did alsoe confesse that shee had
in her 7 or 8 chests of money which came from
Genua in a ffriggott and ws taken into that
shippe at Naples, but they could not tell to
whom that money belonged but as hthey thought
to Gennuizes, and Captaine Woods Purser and Marchant with the Purser
with the Neapolitan shippe wente on
shoare togeather uppon the Mountains and there
told some money, but how much this
examinate knoweth not, for they would not
habe any of the Company to goe alonhe
with them, and after aboute an hower, they returned
aboard Captaine Woods shippe and his
Marchant had his pocketts full of money and
tolde the Company that hee had money for
them for a drinckeinge penny which they
afterwards said was five hundred dollers,
which they paid to the Company at Gio or at sea but since
their comeinge home the said Wood hath stopped yt from
them out of their wages at 4s 8d a doller
And this hee affirmeth uppon his oath to bee
true, Ac aliter nescit

Robart Johnson [SIGNATURE, RH SIDE]

John Newman of Sandwich sailor late one of
the Company of the said shippe sworne as
aforesaide saieth as followeth

2.o ffeb: 1629o stilo etcetera

Thomas Sheriffe de Harewich in Comitatu Essex nauta
annos ahens 34 aut circiter testis et cetera

Ad primum articulum allius in hac parte dat et oblat That the
articulate shippe the Ann Speedwell and her Company in her
did serve in the voyage articulate in whole pay by the space of 8 moneths
videlicet from the 10th of May to the 8th of January
1629. And this hee affirmeth to bee true who was
Master of the said shippe all the tyme aforesaid./

Ad secundum affirmat, That the Ann Speedwell arrived
at Saint Lucars the 19th day fof August 1629 and came
from thence the 28th day of November eodem anno
and in all that tyme the Merchant and his ffactors
mighte and did unlade their goods out of her and might
have reladen goods at such tymes as they pleased
yf they had had goods to have laden, And this
hee affirmeth to bee true of hi sknowledge
reddens reconem scientia sua ut supra Ac aliter
(salvis sunsequen) ad huminoi ac nescit deponere;

Ad tertium affirmat That the Merchant or his ggactors
had noe goods either of his owne or any others that hee
tooke to freighte to relade the Ann Speedwell untill the
the seconde day of November 1629o
the shippe holdeinge there expectinge goods all that
tyme and the Merchant was never denied to unlade
or relade any goods all that tyme, neether did
hee ever offer to lade any goods into her untill
the second day of November aforesaid, And this
hee affirmeth to bee true reddens racoems
scientia sua ut supra./

Ad quartum affirmat That the sailes which were fetcht
from the Ann Speedwell the 30th of Auguste
1629o were onely an old foresaile and an old
foretop saile and that notwithstanding, there
was libertye and freedome for the Merchant Master and
Company to unlade and relade their shippe at
their pleasure accordingly after the fetching
of those sailes on shoare videlicet the 7th 8 and
9th of September laste the [?deales] in the said shipp
were unladen out of her by the order of the Merchants
ffactor, and there were never any of the shipps Company
imprisoned or Guards or other Spaniards putt on
board her but onely a wayter for the custome
whereas in cases of imbargue (as this examinate hath
bin tolde by divers that have bin imbrgued in Spaine
